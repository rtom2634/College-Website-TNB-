Syllabus For LL.B & B.A.LL.B

Course Design

LL.B. Part-I (First Semester)

Course details for the first semester of LL.B. Part-I:

Sl. No. Compulsory Paper Paper Full Marks
1 Jurisprudence I 80
2 Constitutional Law-I II 80
3 Law of Contract III 80
Optional Paper: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Human Rights Law and Practice IV 80
Right to Information
Maritime Law
General English V 80

LL.B. Part-I (Second Semester)

Course details for the second semester of LL.B. Part-I:

Sl. No. Compulsory Papers Paper Full Marks
1 Law of Crimes (Indian Penal Code) I 80
2 Constitutional Law-I II 80
3 Family Law-I (Hindu Law) III 80
Optional Papers: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Women and Criminal Law IV 80
Offences against Child and Juvenile
International Economic Law
Legal Language V 80

LL.B. Part-II (Third Semester)

Course details for the third semester of LL.B. Part-II:

Sl. No. Compulsory Papers Paper Full Marks
1 Family Law-II (Muslim Law) I 80
2 Property Law II 80
3 Law of Torts including MV Accident and Consumer Protection Laws III 80
Optional Papers: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Banking Law IV 80
Equity and Trust
Clinical Paper
Professional Ethics & Professional Accountancy System V 80

LL.B. Part-II (Fourth Semester)

Course details for the fourth semester of LL.B. Part-II:

Sl. No. Compulsory Paper Paper Full Marks
1 Criminal Procedure Code, Juvenile (Care and Protection) Justice Act and Probation of Offenders Acts I 80
2 Public International Law II 80
3 Company Law III 80
Optional Paper: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Humanitarian & Refugee Law IV 80
Information Technology Law
Clinical Paper: Moot Court Exercise and Internship
V 80

LL.B. Part-III (Fifth Semester)

Course details for the fifth semester of LL.B. Part-III:

Sl. No. Compulsory Papers Paper Full Marks
1 Civil Procedure Code and Limitation Act I 80
2 Law of Evidence II 80
3 Administrative Law III 80
Optional Paper: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Patent Law IV 80
Intellectual Property Rights Law & I.P.R. Litigation
Clinical Paper: Drafting, Pleading and Conveyance
V 80

LL.B. Part-III (Sixth Semester)

Course details for the sixth semester of LL.B. Part-III:

Sl. No. Compulsory Papers Paper Full Marks
1 Principles of Taxation I 80
2 Environmental Law II 80
3 Labour Law III 80
Optional Papers: Any one of the following subjects is to be opted as the 4th Paper.
Insurance Law IV 80
Competition Law
Land Tenure
Clinical Paper: Alternate Dispute Resolutions
V 80

Honours Groups

Details of the Honours Groups:

A. Constitutional Law Group

  • Indian Federalism
  • Affirmative Action and Discriminative Justice
  • Comparative Constitution
  • Gender Justice and Feminist Jurisprudence
  • Law on Education
  • Civil Society and Public Grievance
  • Local Self Government including Panchayat Administration
  • Health Law
  • Citizenship and Emigration Law
  • Legislative Drafting
  • Judicial Review

B. International Law Group

  • International Organisation
  • International Human Rights
  • Private International Law
  • International Environmental Law
  • International Labour Organisation and Labour Laws
  • International Dispute Resolution Bodies
  • Law of Sea and International River
  • International Criminal Law and International Criminal Court
  • IMF and World Bank
  • Regional Agreement & Regionalization
  • UNCITRAL Model Codes
  • Maritime Law

C. Intellectual Property Law Group

  • Copyright
  • Trademark and Design
  • Trade Secret and Technology Transfer
  • IPR Litigation
  • Life Patent
  • Bio Diversity Protection
  • Information Technology
  • IPR in Pharma Industry
  • Patent Drafting and Specification Writing
  • Farmers and Breeders Right
  • IPR in SMEs

D. Business Law Group

  • Law and Economics
  • Investment Law
  • Financial Market Regulation
  • Foreign Trade
  • Law of Carriage
  • Transportation Law
  • Bankruptcy and Insolvency
  • Corporate Governance
  • Merger and Acquisition
  • Direct Taxation
  • Indirect Taxation
  • Law on Corporate Finance
  • Law on Infrastructure Development
  • Special Contract

E. International Trade Law

  • International Trade Economics

  • Dumping and Countervailing Duty

  • Cross Border Investment

  • Dispute Resolution
